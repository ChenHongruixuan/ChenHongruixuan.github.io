Research

My research uses machine learning and computer vision for Earth observation. The work groups into four directions: interpreting multi-sensor EO data, designing EO-specialised architectures, training under limited annotation, and extending EO capabilities with foundation models. The interest started during a year as a trainee at the UN Satellite Centre (UNOSAT) and now spans disaster response, urban planning, land-cover mapping, and environmental monitoring.

Multi-sensor EO data interpretation

Optical, SAR, maps, and time series each capture the Earth's surface in different ways. I study what they share and where they diverge, so models stay reliable when one sensor is unavailable.

Architectures shaped for EO

Standard vision models miss what makes EO data different: multi-temporal, multi-spectral, very large in spatial extent. I design networks that build these properties into their structure.

Training with fewer labels

Pixel-level annotation is expensive and slow. I work on open benchmarks, label-efficient learning, and synthetic data, so models can be trained faster and used in regions or scenarios where labels are missing.

Foundation models for geoscience

A more recent thread looks at foundation models and vision-language systems for geoscience, and whether they can move EO from producing maps to supporting higher-level reasoning across domains such as land-cover mapping, urban planning, disaster response, and environmental monitoring.
